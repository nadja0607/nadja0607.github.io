About

Let me introduce myself.

It has been about ten years since I learned my first magic trick. The normal routine is that after picking a card, the magician shuffles the deck, and abracadabra, your card is revealed. Although I’ve always loved to solve puzzles and the logical and creative thinking that goes into them, it took me four years at New York University to acknowledge that as a ten-year-old I was already developing my own algorithms. I didn’t realize for a long time that this is what computer scientists do every day – they create the illusion of the impossible.

As my desire to discover more has challenged me, I look forward to incredible opportunities bringing unpredictable experiences that will propel me to deepen my professional interests surrounded by lots of fun and positive energy. What we have in common is much more than programming. We invent, create, design, and build - just like magicians with lines of code being our magic spells. I want to graduate thinking, not that I’m done, but that there is so much more to do. We can agree that the real world is much more than lectures from the book and exams we take. Theory and knowledge matter but it’s practice and passion that make perfect. Never have I thought of being this close to understanding the secret of how to create my illusion of impossible.
